<p>The&nbsp;<a href="http://smart.mit.edu/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Singapore-MIT Alliance for Research and Technology (SMART)</a>&nbsp;is
a leading research centre in Asia established by the Massachusetts Institute
of Technology (MIT) and the National Research Foundation (NRF) of Singapore
to identify and conduct research on critical problems of societal significance
for today and the future.</p>
<p>Exemplifying the MIT spirit of research excellence and Singapore’s commitment
to R&amp;D, SMART advances high-impact research within and beyond CREATE,
building strategic partnerships with universities, research institutes,
hospitals, government agencies and industry.</p>
<p>SMART fosters a creative and collaborative environment that stimulates
major discoveries through its interdisciplinary structure. Its three Interdisciplinary
Research Groups (IRGs) are as follows:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p><a href="https://amr.smart.mit.edu/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR)</a>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>AMR leverages the scientific and clinical strengths of MIT and Singapore
to develop transformative technologies to identify, respond to, and treat
drug-resistant microbes.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="http://camp.smart.mit.edu/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Critical Analytics for Manufacturing Personalized-Medicine (CAMP)</a>&nbsp;</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>CAMP is an Interdisciplinary Research Group in Singapore and at MIT focusing
on better ways to produce living cells as medicine delivered to humans.
CAMP launched in June 2019, with the goal to address two key bottlenecks
facing production of a range of potential cell therapies: critical quality
attributes (CQA) and process analytic technologies (PAT) for cell therapy
production.&nbsp;</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="https://distap.mit.edu/" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Disruptive and Sustainable Technologies for Agricultural Precision (DISTAP)</a>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Aims to revolutionalize how food is produced to meet the demands of a
growing population in an increasing resource constrained world.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="https://smart.mit.edu/research/lees/about-lees" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Low Energy Electronic Systems (LEES)</a>
</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Developing new integrated circuits with reduced energy per function for
applications in wireless communication, power electronics, LED lighting,
printing, displays and computing</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>SMART also consists of the&nbsp;<a href="https://smart.mit.edu/innovation-centre/our-program" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">SMART Innovation Centre</a>&nbsp;-
the translational research arm of SMART - which is modelled after MIT’s
Deshpande Center. It uses Adaptive Innovation to assist researchers from
Singapore to pursue novel avenues of market-driven research and helps accelerate
their innovations toward commercialisation.</p>
